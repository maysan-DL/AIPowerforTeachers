Definition

What is Artificial Intelligence?

The science of teaching machines to mimic intelligent human behaviours. Unlike traditional software, AI systems are taught and trained using computer programming that learns and adapts over time.

Machine Learning (ML)

A method of teaching machines to learn and improve autonomously from massive amounts of data.

Core Principles

Ethical Use of AI in Education

Navigating the challenges of AI implementation while ensuring a safe learning environment.

Hallucination

AI can sometimes generate facts that sound convincing but are entirely incorrect.

Bias & Fairness

AI reflects biases from its training data. We must ensure outputs do not promote stereotypes.

Data Privacy

Never input Personally Identifiable Information (PII) into open AI platforms.

Academic Integrity

Focus on the process. Use AI for brainstorming, ensuring final work reflects student mastery.

Instructional Input

What is a Prompt?

A prompt is the input or instruction given to an AI model to guide its output.

Different ways to prompt AI

1. Type Text

Directly communicate instructions or questions to the AI.

"Draft a 3-day lesson plan for Year 5 students about the water cycle."

Anatomy of a Prompt

Follow these 5 components for high-quality results.

1. Persona

Who is the AI acting as? (e.g. Science Teacher)

2. Aim

What is the clear objective? (e.g. Write a quiz)

3. Recipients

Who is the audience? (e.g. Year 9 students)

4. Theme

What is the tone? (e.g. Fun & engaging)

5. Structure

Desired format? (e.g. A table)

Sample Prompt

"You are an experienced Biology teacher. Create a quiz for high school students about photosynthesis. Use a professional tone and present the output in a bulleted list."

Refining the Conversation

The Iteration Cycle

Prompting is a dialogue, not a one-time command.

Step 1
Evaluate

Check if the output meets your goals. Is it too long? Too complex? Missing key data?

Step 2
Adjust

Add new constraints. "Add a glossary," "Simplify for Year 3," or "Focus on Chapter 2."

Step 3
Iterate

Reply to the AI. "Great, now turn that quiz into a lesson plan table."

Pro Tips

Specific Prompts = Better Outputs

Vague

"Write a lesson about history."

Specific

"Write a 45-minute lesson plan for 14-year-olds about the industrial revolution, including a 5-minute warm-up activity and a closing debate prompt."

The more context you provide, the less "hallucination" occurs.
