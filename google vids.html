What is Google Vids?

Google Vids is a new AI-powered video creation tool integrated into Google Workspace. It simplifies the video production process by helping you generate scripts, storyboards, and even complete video drafts using assets from your Google Drive.

"Help me create a video"

AI Storyboarding

Enter a prompt, and Vids creates an initial storyboard with suggested scenes and media.

Script Generation

Automatically generate a script based on your documents or a simple description.

Stock Library

Access millions of high-quality stock videos, images, and background music tracks.

AI Voiceovers

Choose from a variety of natural-sounding AI voices to narrate your project.

Activity: The AI Storyboard

Use AI to generate a full storyboard for a school project or lesson.

  • Open Google Vids from your Google Workspace.
  • Select "Help me create a video".
  • Enter your topic and desired goal.
  • Tip: In Step 4 of the creation wizard, toggle the "Style" options to find a visual theme that matches your subject.

Sample Prompt:

Create a 2-minute video for 6th-grade students about the importance of water conservation. Make it upbeat, educational, and include a call to action at the end.

Activity: Multi-Modal Scripting

Generate a script by pulling information from a specific Google Doc or Slide deck.

  • Start a new video project.
  • Choose "Use a file from Drive" in the prompt box.
  • Select a document containing your lesson notes.
  • Tip: After the script is generated, use the "Voiceover" menu to preview different AI accents to find the one that sounds most professional.

Activity: Final Project Draft

Finalize your draft by adding transitions and background music.

  • Refine your scenes by clicking "Replace Media".
  • Add background music from the Stock Library.
  • Share the video link with a colleague for feedback.
  • Tip: Use the "Recording" feature if you want to record your own face/screen alongside the AI content for a personal touch.

Google Vids lowers the barrier to video production, allowing educators and students to focus on storytelling and content rather than complex editing software.
